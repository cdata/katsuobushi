## What happened

`Board::parse` preserves only two regions verbatim: the `preamble`
(everything up to the first lane) and the trailing `%% kanban:settings`
block. Anything else that is not a lane heading, a card list-item, the
archive, or the settings block is consumed as a "stray line" in the lane
loop (`i += 1`) and never stored — so it is silently dropped on the next
`to_text` rewrite.

The most reachable case: free prose (or any non-card block) placed after
the last lane's cards on a board that has **no** `%% kanban:settings`
block. A CLI mutation re-emits the board without it.

## Reproduction

1. A BOARD.md ending with `## To-do\n\n- [ ] [[a3f7b2]]\n\nSome human note`
   and no settings block.
2. `project status set a3f7b2 in-progress` (or any mutation).
3. Result: `Some human note` is gone from the rewritten board.

## Expected

Either preserve unrecognized trailing content verbatim (like the preamble
and settings), or have `lint` warn that the board carries content the
writer cannot round-trip. Silent data loss is the concern.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Trailing non-lane content survives a CLI rewrite, OR `lint` flags it
- [ ] A round-trip of such a board is loss-free (or explicitly reported)
